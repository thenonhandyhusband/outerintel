03 · Executive Summary

The Operators Winning in AI Search Are Not Who You'd Expect

Med spa owners in every market are spending real money on Facebook and Instagram ads to drive awareness. The ads work. Prospects get interested and do what every consumer does next: they open Google to verify before committing. What they find in those thirty seconds — whether a rich, credible, well-managed review profile or a thin, stale one — determines whether they call. Most operators have no precise picture of what that verification moment looks like. This report measures it.

Stars are table stakes, not a competitive advantage.
69% of the 131 businesses in this study average 4.9 stars or higher. The entire competitive field is compressed into a 0.3-point band at the top of a five-star scale. Any strategy built around improving a star rating is chasing a metric that has already hit its ceiling.

The gap between top and bottom performers is not a gap in customer experience or clinical quality. The top quartile and bottom quartile are nearly identical on star rating (4.93 vs. 4.87), sentiment (9.28 vs. 9.15), and review specificity. They are dramatically different on review volume (299 vs. 79) and response rate (92.2% vs. 38.3%).

The operators winning in AI search are not the ones with the best reviews.
They are the ones who have built the most disciplined review infrastructure. A business can score in the top 20 nationally on AI Visibility while its reputation is quietly deteriorating — the star rating masks what AI engines actually read.
04 · The AI Search Opportunity

The Verification Moment Has Changed. Most Operators Haven't.

Five years ago the verification moment was simple: a prospect searched Google, saw a list of businesses with star ratings, and chose the one with the most reviews or the highest rating. Today the process is more sophisticated.

When a prospect searches for a med spa in their city, they encounter AI-generated summaries alongside traditional listings. Google's AI Overviews synthesize information across a business's review profile to produce a narrative description: what services the business offers, what patients consistently say, whether staff is praised by name, and whether there are recurring concerns. The prospect receives a constructed portrait — not just a star rating.

97% of consumers research services online before booking.
BrightLocal, 2025. The ad creates intent. The review profile either captures it or surrenders it to a better-positioned competitor.

The Five Signals AI Systems Weigh

Signal 01
Volume & Freshness

Total reviews + recency establish credibility. A large count that has gone quiet reads differently than a steady ongoing stream.

Signal 02
Velocity

AI systems favor businesses generating new reviews consistently, not large historical counts that have stopped growing.

Signal 03
Owner Responses

Responses are indexed content that AI systems read and weigh. They function as a freshness and engagement signal.

Signal 04
Review Content

The actual language customers use matters. Generic praise and specific outcomes are not equivalent to an AI system.

Signal 05
Platform Consistency

Consistency of signals across platforms matters. Google accounts for the majority of discovery search and AI Overview sourcing.

The Bottom Line

A practice spending $2,000/month on Meta ads with an unmanaged review profile is driving warm prospects directly to better-positioned competitors.

10 · The 5 Gaps Most Operators Are Leaving Open

The Same Gaps Appear Across Every Market

Every market in this study contains businesses with strong clinical reputations and weak AI visibility. In most cases the reason is not mysterious.

Gap 01 · Velocity
No Systematic Review Collection Process

Reviews arrive when patients remember to leave them — at a fraction of what a deliberate system produces. The top quartile earns 9.82 reviews/month. The bottom quartile earns 2.38. That gap compounds to nearly 90 additional reviews per year. The practices at 1–2 reviews per month are not just behind today. They are receding.

Gap 02 · Response
20% of Businesses Respond to Fewer Than 10% of Reviews

Owner responses are indexed content. Every response adds the operator's voice, contributes keyword context, and signals to AI systems that the business is actively managed. A business not responding is handing its entire narrative to its customers and leaving indexed content on the table.

Gap 03 · Negative Review Response
16 Businesses Respond to 0% of Negative Reviews

A one-star review with no owner response sits in the public record indefinitely. It does not disappear because the business ignores it. It compounds, particularly if the complaint pattern recurs. The absence of a response is itself a signal — and not a positive one.

Gap 04 · Specificity
Only 38.7% of Reviews Name a Specific Service

Only 29.4% describe a concrete outcome. Operators who coach patients toward specific, service-naming, outcome-describing reviews build content that functions as genuine AI signal. This gap will widen as AI search systems mature.

Gap 05 · Visibility
Operating Without Competitive Intelligence

Almost no operator has a structured, data-driven picture of where they rank on velocity, response rate, content quality, and AI sentiment relative to the top businesses in their market. Without knowing where you stand, it is difficult to prioritize what to fix, impossible to measure whether interventions are working, and easy to believe a 4.9 rating means everything is fine. You cannot close gaps you cannot see.
