What The Data Shows

The same five gaps appear in every market.

Outer Intel analyzed 131 independent med spas across 12 US cities. The businesses falling behind on AI visibility share the same patterns regardless of city size, market maturity, or clinical quality. These are the gaps.

Gap 01

No Systematic Review Collection Process

Most operators earn reviews when patients remember to leave them. Top-quartile businesses earn 9.82 reviews per month on average. Bottom-quartile businesses earn 2.38. That gap compounds to nearly 90 additional reviews per year and the businesses at the bottom are not just behind today, they are receding.

Gap 02

Owner Responses Are Being Skipped

Twenty percent of businesses in this study respond to fewer than 10% of their reviews. Owner responses are indexed content. Every response adds the operator's voice, contributes keyword context, and signals to AI systems that the business is actively managed. Eleven businesses respond to 0%.

Gap 03

Negative Reviews Sit Unanswered

Sixteen businesses in this study respond to 0% of their negative reviews. A one-star review with no owner response sits in the public record as the final word. It does not disappear because the business ignores it.

Gap 04

Reviews Are Too Generic to Signal Anything

Only 38.7% of reviews name a specific service and only 29.4% describe a concrete outcome. "Great experience, highly recommend" does not answer "who does the best Botox in Dallas?" A review that names the practitioner, the treatment, and the result does.

Gap 05

No Competitive Baseline Exists

Most owners know their own rating. Almost none have a structured picture of where they rank on velocity, response rate, and AI sentiment relative to the top businesses in their market. You cannot close gaps you cannot see.
